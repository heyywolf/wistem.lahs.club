Initiatives

We are currently working on a few projects that are founded and led by a few club officers and members. These projects aid the fulfillment of achieving our mission through our two purposes: reaching back and looking forward. The names and descriptions of these initiatives can be found below.

STEMpower

STEMpower is a volunteer organization based in the Bay Area and works with WiSTEM, a club focused on empowering students in high school to pursue STEM. Hosting a series of workshops for the younger generation, elementary school students are able to gain exposure to STEM through innovation and creativity. Through hands-on exploration and team projects, students will have the opportunity to explore topics ranging from water density experiments to Python coding.

STEM Speakers

The STEM Speaker Series brings in at least one speaker per month to talk to students about their career in STEM. The speakers come from all fields of STEM and can be in different stages of their career to help high schoolers holistically visualize their future in STEM. The goal of STEM Speakers is to present students with a role model and an example of what they can achieve. Being able to personally connect with someone in their field of interest provides a new perspective on STEM and can also spark a new passion. By providing these opportunities to connect and converse with students and professionals in the STEM field, high schoolers can visualize an exciting future in STEM.

Learn more

Mentorship Program

The goal of this program is to provide students with the guidance and support they need to develop a strong, well-rounded academic and extracurricular profile while further exploring their interests as they navigate throughout their 4 years of high school. This mentorship program will pair college undergraduates with high school students (freshman to juniors) interested in pursuing a career in STEM. Mentors will provide mentees with advice ranging from how to succeed in challenging classes and build strong relationships with teachers, to how to effectively communicate and seek out opportunities outside of the classroom. In addition, mentor-mentee pairings provide an opportunity for participants to build strong, personal relationships that extend beyond the scope of this program.
